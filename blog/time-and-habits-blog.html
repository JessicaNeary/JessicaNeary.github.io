2th October 16 - Blog Entry 2


Time and Habits

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I summarised the main points I resonated with from each link into a set of notes, so have essentially been working with an almagamation of them. I have been trying to meditate more and timebox everything, as well as ignoring my lizard brain and give myself the conditions necessary to achieve flow. I have tried the pomorado technique in the past, but did not find it was for me, as frequent breaks disturb my workflow.

What did you find interesting?

Learning how mediation actually affects the brain was very interesting. I also enjoyed reading the Growth Mindset notes on how confidence is just displayed ability.

What worked for you?

I found the Counter-intuitive Productivity techniques very helpful. Reminding myself that I was doing something interesting and workwhile stopped me from falling into the procrastinators mindset. I didn't scold myself for taking too long breaks, knowing it would improve my mindset and improve my focus when I did return to work. The waffle cards helped me break the workload into nice, non-intimidating chunks, and I found by changing what I was working on every couple hours I avoided getting bored and losing flow.

Which will you experiment further with?

I will reread my productivity notes every so often and work to apply the behavior techniques to all aspects of my life. I need to remember to keep things small and not get overwhelmed by a bigger picture, doing things because they are interesting rather than with the aim of getting really good at them. I will also make an effort to meditate often and use a laptop instead of desktop so I can only have so much running at a time and can physically take myself away from distractions.
